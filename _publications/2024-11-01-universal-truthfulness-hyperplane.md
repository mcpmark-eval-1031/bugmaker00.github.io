This paper investigates the existence of a universal truthfulness hyperplane inside large language
models (LLMs). We explore the geometric structure underlying truthfulness representations in LLMs
and demonstrate that a single linear hyperplane can separate truthful from untruthful statements
across diverse topics and model families. Our findings provide insights into how LLMs internally
represent and reason about factual information.
