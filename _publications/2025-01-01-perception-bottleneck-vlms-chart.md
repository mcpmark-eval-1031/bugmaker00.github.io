This paper analyzes the perception bottleneck of vision-language models (VLMs) when applied to
chart understanding tasks. We systematically evaluate current state-of-the-art VLMs on a
comprehensive chart understanding benchmark and identify that perception — rather than reasoning
— is the primary bottleneck. Our analysis reveals specific visual understanding failures and we
propose targeted approaches to overcome these limitations.
